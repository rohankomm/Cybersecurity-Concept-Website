CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a model for security policies and practices that includes:

  • Confidentiality: Ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized people.
  • Integrity: Ensures data remains accurate and trustworthy.
  • Availability: Ensures authorized users have access to the data and systems they need.
Three States of Data

Data can exist in three main states:

  • Data at Rest: Data stored on a device, server, or database.
  • Data in Transit: Data being transmitted across networks.
  • Data in Use: Data actively being used or processed.
Types of Authentication

There are multiple types of authentication methods:

  • Password-Based: Basic authentication using a password.
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security.
  • Biometric Authentication: Uses fingerprint, facial recognition, etc.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Combines multiple authentication methods.
Passwords

Strong passwords are essential to protect data and accounts:

  • Use complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid common words or easily guessable information.
  • Use password managers for safe storage of passwords.
Phishing

Phishing is a common method used to steal information:

  • Watch for suspicious emails or messages asking for sensitive information.
  • Check URLs and email addresses for authenticity.
  • Do not click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.
Potential Threats

Cyber threats can affect individuals and companies alike:

  • Malware: Software designed to damage or disrupt systems.
  • Ransomware: Malware that demands payment to unlock data.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating individuals to reveal confidential information.
